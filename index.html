Hanging Paralell Robot Suspended by Wire

This system is suspended from a level ceiling using four seperate lines. The robot features four winches on each of its corners. As these winches draw and release line the robot moves in its environment. Idividual winch setpoints are produced through inverse kinematic functions written in MATLAB. These setpoints are sent serially to the ESP32 microprocessor which performs rapid calculations as the PID controller.

Balancing Bike Using a PID Controlled Rear Fan

The bike levatates its rear spar using the thrust produced by a low pitch propeller. Thrust produced in the rear can then be redirected by a small rudder and turn the robot. The drive wheels are nested beneath the hood of the vehicle and are driven by a highly geared brushless motor. The rear fan's velocity is PID controlled with an onboard IMU. This system has been tuned so that stability is held even when the bike moves horizontally. The bike was programmed in C++ and implements a handful of saftey features including the detection of a dangerous orientation, low battery voltage, and high current draw. If any of these are discovered the system will enter an emergency stopped state.
